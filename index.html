“This is how I define man up: you are what you are, and the sooner you stop hating what makes you unique and start celebrating it and using it to make you stand out from the crowd, the better your life will be.” ― Ross Mathews, Man Up!: Tales of My Delusional Self-Confidence

02

Ross Mathews used to be an intern for "The Tonight Show with Jay Leno" during his college days. This experience eventually led to him becoming a regular guest and correspondent on the show, where he became known for his enthusiastic personality and quick wit. In addition to his on-screen work, Ross Mathews is also an author and has released two books. Besides "Man Up!: Tales of My Delusional Self-Confidence," he published another memoir titled "Name Drop" in 2020, where he shared stories about his encounters with celebrities throughout his career.
